"Embracing the 'New Run': A Journey of Resilience and Triumph at the Edinburgh Half Marathon"

May 28, 2023, 23:06 PM

A month ago, under the weight of tremendous stress, I turned to running as a form of solace. To ensure that I stayed committed to this journey, I enrolled myself in the Edinburgh Half Marathon.

My journey began on April 25th with a modest 3km run - an endeavor filled with uncertainty, exhilaration, and a sense of accomplishment. Fast forward to yesterday, I realized a personal triumph: I completed my first half marathon, covering a challenging distance of 21.08km in 2:02:13.

As I traversed the halfway mark of my run, an empowering realization struck me: I chose to christen this journey as my 'new run'. The idea of running long distances was an alien concept to me until yesterday. Yet, there I was, on a profound exploration of my physical capabilities and pushing the limits of what I thought was possible.

This marathon journey has been about much more than running. It's been seven years since I last took running seriously. Those years have been a tumultuous roller-coaster ride, marked by a fierce battle against a chronic illness, enduring numerous emotional upheavals, and confronting life's daunting challenges head-on.

During this journey, 'Neuron' has held a special place in my heart. As a symbol of my unwavering academic pursuit, it's been a beacon of hope that has guided me through the darkest times.

I am fortunate to have recovered from my illness, but the post-recovery phase has been a struggle, trying to rebuild my life after such a formidable adversary. But with determination, perseverance, and resilience, I've bravely continued chasing the life I desire.

Approaching the finish line of the half marathon, a surge of emotions washed over me. On the brink of tears, I felt a profound sense of pride in my resilience and courage. Running this half marathon has proved to me that the disease did not - and will not - defeat me. It has shown me that I am not only strong enough to embrace life's challenges but also to live life on my terms.

The 'new run' signifies my resolve to move forward, embracing the promise of new beginnings, and facing the future with strength and optimism. The Edinburgh Half Marathon was a pivotal step on this journey, marking not just a personal achievement, but a victory over my past challenges and a beacon for a brighter, bolder future.

And so, the 'new run' continues...
